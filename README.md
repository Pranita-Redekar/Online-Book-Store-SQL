<h1 align="center">📚 Online Book Store - PostgreSQL Project</h1>

<p align="center">
  This project demonstrates the implementation of an Online Book Store database using PostgreSQL. 
  It focuses on managing books, customers, and orders while performing insightful queries 
  to analyze sales, inventory, and customer behavior.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>📌 Project Overview</h2>
<p>
The Online Book Store system is designed to handle book inventory, customer details, 
and order transactions using PostgreSQL. The project showcases how relational databases 
can efficiently store and manage data while enabling powerful querying for business insights.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>🛠️ Skills Used</h2>

<ul>
  <li>PostgreSQL</li>
  <li>Joins (JOIN, LEFT JOIN)</li>
  <li>Aggregate Functions (SUM, AVG, COUNT)</li>
  <li>Group By & Having</li>
  <li>Subqueries</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>📊 Queries Performed</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Retrieve all books in the "Fiction" genre</li>
  <li>Find books published after the year 1950</li>
  <li>List all customers from Canada</li>
  <li>Show orders placed in November 2023</li>
  <li>Retrieve the total stock of books available</li>
  <li>Find details of the most expensive book</li>
  <li>Show customers who ordered more than 1 quantity</li>
  <li>Retrieve orders where total amount exceeds $20</li>
  <li>List all genres available in the Books table</li>
  <li>Find the book with the lowest stock</li>
  <li>Calculate total revenue generated from all orders</li>
  <li>Retrieve total number of books sold for each genre</li>
  <li>Find the average price of books in the "Fantasy" genre</li>
  <li>List customers who placed at least 2 orders</li>
  <li>Find the most frequently ordered book</li>
  <li>Show top 3 most expensive books in "Fantasy" genre</li>
  <li>Retrieve total quantity of books sold by each author</li>
  <li>List cities where customers spent over $30</li>
  <li>Find the customer who spent the most on orders</li>
  <li>Calculate stock remaining after fulfilling all orders</li>
</ul>
